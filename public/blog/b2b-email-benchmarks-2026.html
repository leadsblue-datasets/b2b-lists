B2B Cold Email Benchmarks 2026: Open Rates, Reply Rates, and Conversion by Industry

October 1, 2025·8 min read·~601 words

Cold email benchmark data is widely published but rarely sourced from B2B-specific outreach. Consumer email benchmarks (Mailchimp's 21% industry averages) are not cold email benchmarks — they measure opted-in subscriber lists, not cold outreach to purchased databases. These benchmarks are derived from B2B cold email campaign analysis across major sending platforms.

Overall B2B Cold Email Benchmarks 2026

Aggregate cold email benchmarks across all industries and geographies for well-targeted B2B campaigns: open rate 19–26%, reply rate 3–7%, positive reply rate (interested responses only) 30–50% of all replies, meeting booked rate from positive reply 35–55%, hard bounce rate below 2% (from verified lists). These are benchmarks for campaigns meeting minimum quality standards — verified email lists, targeted ICP, professional copy, working opt-out. Poorly targeted campaigns with unverified lists will perform significantly below these figures.

Benchmarks by Industry Vertical

Technology and SaaS targets: open rate 22–30%, reply rate 4–9%. Technology buyers are accustomed to vendor outreach and make fast evaluation decisions. Specificity about their tech stack or product dramatically outperforms generic outreach in this vertical.

Healthcare and Medical Practices: open rate 16–22%, reply rate 2–5%. Lower open rates because healthcare professionals have heavy email volume and sophisticated spam filtering. Practice management and efficiency framing outperforms clinical claims. Independent practice owners respond significantly better than employed physicians.

Manufacturing and Engineering: open rate 18–24%, reply rate 2–5%. More conservative buyers with longer evaluation cycles. Operational metrics (uptime improvement, defect reduction) dramatically outperform abstract value propositions. German and Japanese manufacturing contacts are in the lower range.

Finance and Banking: open rate 14–20%, reply rate 2–4%. Heavy compliance filtering at financial institutions blocks many cold emails. Regulatory framing and specific credential presentation improve results significantly. Community bank decision-makers are more accessible than enterprise bank buyers.

Professional Services (Legal, Consulting, Accounting): open rate 17–23%, reply rate 2–5%. Relationship-dependent buyers who respond to very specific vertical expertise signals. Generic professional services pitches perform poorly; deep practice-area specificity performs well.

Benchmarks by Geography

United States: open rate 20–28%, reply rate 3–8%. The most outreach-receptive major market. English is business standard. Decision-making moves faster than European equivalents. Best results from Tuesday–Thursday sends, 7:30–9:30am local time.

United Kingdom: open rate 16–22%, reply rate 2–5%. Professional but somewhat reserved. LinkedIn warm-up before cold email increases reply rates by 25–35%. Friday is noticeably dead for outreach.

Germany: open rate 12–18%, reply rate 1.5–3.5%. The most conservative major market. German language outperforms English by 18–24%. Reply to meeting conversion is the highest of any market — German decision-makers who reply are genuinely interested.

India: open rate 20–28%, reply rate 3–8%. Matches the US in responsiveness. WhatsApp follow-up increases conversion significantly. Monday–Wednesday is peak engagement.

Singapore: open rate 20–26%, reply rate 3–7%. Highest open rates in Southeast Asia. Fast decision cycles. English universal in corporate contacts.

Australia: open rate 16–22%, reply rate 2–5%. Inferred consent available under Spam Act for published business email. Direct copy outperforms corporate language.

What Kills Performance Below Benchmarks

Four factors that consistently pull actual campaign performance below these benchmarks: (1) Unverified email lists — hard bounce rates above 3% damage sender reputation and reduce subsequent campaign deliverability. Always verify before importing. (2) Too-broad targeting — a campaign to 'all technology companies in the US' produces 50% of the reply rates of a campaign to 'US SaaS companies with 50–500 employees targeting SME clients'. Narrow targeting compounds performance. (3) Generic copy — the first sentence determines whether the email is read beyond the opening. A subject line referencing the recipient's specific product or market outperforms generic templates 3:1 on reply rates. (4) Wrong timing — sending to a German manufacturing executive in August (holiday month) or to an accountant in March (tax season) will produce near-zero results regardless of copy quality.
