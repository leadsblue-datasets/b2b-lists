How to Target Shopify Users: Complete B2B Outreach Guide

March 10, 2026·8 min read·~629 words

Shopify powers over 4.5 million active stores globally and has become the dominant e-commerce platform for direct-to-consumer brands at every stage from startup to mid-market. Understanding how to reach Shopify merchants effectively — and how to segment the market by revenue tier, technology stack, and category — determines whether your outreach achieves sub-2% or above-6% reply rates.

The Shopify Market: Size, Segments, and Opportunity

The Shopify merchant base is highly stratified. Approximately 80% of Shopify stores generate under $100,000 in annual GMV. The commercially relevant segment for most B2B vendors is the 15 to 20% of stores generating $100,000 to $5 million annually — roughly 600,000 to 800,000 stores globally. Above $5 million annually, merchants typically operate on Shopify Plus (approximately 30,000 to 50,000 merchants globally) with more sophisticated technology requirements.

The sub-$100K segment is generally not worth targeting for B2B software unless your product has a very low price point and a self-serve model. The $100K to $5M segment is the sweet spot for most B2B vendors: the founder or operations manager is directly reachable, has budget, makes decisions quickly, and has enough operational complexity to benefit from tooling.

List Sources: How to Identify Shopify Merchants

Three primary data sources for Shopify merchant outreach. Technology platform databases compiled by BuiltWith identify websites using Shopify by detecting Shopify-specific HTML patterns, CDN fingerprints, and JavaScript resources. B2B Data Index catalogs BuiltWith-sourced Shopify merchant lists with verified contact information at the Shopify database page. Coverage: approximately 500,000 to 800,000 Shopify merchants with business email contacts, segmented by geography, industry category, and traffic tier.

Commerce data providers provide merchant-level revenue estimates and traffic analytics that allow segmentation by GMV range. Combining technology database contact data with commerce data revenue estimates produces the most precisely targeted Shopify merchant lists.

Segmentation: The Four Shopify Cohorts

Cohort 1 — Emerging (under $500K GMV): founder-led, tight budgets, self-serve tooling preference, high reply rates to very specific observations about their current setup. Decision-maker is the founder. Cohort 2 — Growing ($500K to $2M GMV): founder still involved but operational managers beginning to emerge; budget for tooling exists. Decision-maker is founder or Head of Operations. Cohort 3 — Scaling ($2M to $10M GMV): dedicated operational team; multiple vendors under evaluation. Decision-maker is Head of Growth or VP Marketing for growth tools; COO for operations. Cohort 4 — Shopify Plus ($10M+ GMV): enterprise-grade procurement; multi-stakeholder evaluation; longer sales cycles. Decision-maker is C-suite with VP-level champion.

Copy That Works for Shopify Merchants

Shopify merchant copy must lead with a specific observation that proves you actually looked at their store. Generic e-commerce copy is immediately dismissed by merchants who receive multiple vendor emails daily. Effective openers reference a specific gap observed in their current setup with a quantified revenue opportunity attached. The observation must be specific and accurate — if you claim to have noticed something that is not actually true about their store, the email is immediately dismissed as templated fabrication.

Subject lines that work for Shopify merchants: [Brand] — one thing I noticed or Shopify merchants at $[X]M — quick question. Keep body copy under 80 words. Shopify merchants are time-poor operators who read email between operational tasks. Long emails are not read. The call to action should be a single low-friction request.

Technology Stack Targeting Within the Shopify Ecosystem

Shopify merchants use a stack of apps alongside the core platform. Targeting merchants who use or do not use specific apps reveals their operational maturity and current vendor relationships. Merchants using Klaviyo indicate marketing sophistication and an existing email programme. Merchants using Recharge already have a subscription model; targeting them for subscription solutions is wasted. Merchants using ReConvert indicate awareness of post-purchase revenue.

Stack-based targeting is available through BuiltWith databases and enhanced Shopify merchant lists. A list of Shopify merchants using Klaviyo but not a post-purchase upsell app is a highly specific, high-conversion audience for post-purchase revenue tools.
