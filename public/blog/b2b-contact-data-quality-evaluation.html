B2B Contact Data Quality: How to Evaluate Any Database

April 14, 2026·9 min read·~654 words

The B2B data market includes providers ranging from globally trusted databases with rigorous verification to low-cost lists of questionable provenance that will damage your sender reputation within a week. The framework for distinguishing them requires evaluating five dimensions before committing to any purchase: verification methodology, coverage depth, data freshness, compliance documentation, and sample testing results.

Dimension 1: Verification Methodology

Email verification methodology determines the deliverability of the data. Three standards exist in the market. SMTP validation: connects to the recipient mail server and checks whether the address exists without sending a message. This is the minimum acceptable standard. Inbox confirmation testing: actually sends a test message to a seed address and confirms delivery to inbox versus spam — a higher standard that also tests deliverability. Domain and registry cross-referencing: cross-checks email domains against active company registrations to identify companies that have closed.

Ask explicitly: what is your verification methodology? When was the last verification cycle? What is your bounce rate guarantee? A provider that cannot answer these questions specifically is a red flag. A provider that claims 95%+ verification should be tested with a sample run through NeverBounce or ZeroBounce before committing to the full list.

Dimension 2: Sample Testing — The Non-Negotiable Step

Before purchasing any list, request a sample of 100 to 200 records from your specific target segment. All reputable providers accommodate sample requests — refusal to provide samples is itself a red flag. Run the sample through NeverBounce, ZeroBounce, or Bouncer independently. Compare your independent verification results to the provider claimed verification rate.

Target thresholds for sample testing: below 5% invalid addresses for enterprise-grade quality; below 8% for acceptable quality with re-verification before use; above 10% invalid addresses from a sample indicates either poor data quality or highly decayed data and should result in rejecting the full purchase.

Dimension 3: Coverage Depth Assessment

Coverage claims are easy to make and difficult to verify without benchmarks. Ask for record counts broken down by: geographic sub-region, industry vertical, job seniority level, and company size range. Compare the provided counts to known market sizes. The UK has approximately 600,000 companies with 10 or more employees. A finance-sector database claiming 2 million UK finance contacts contains significant inflation, as the finance sector employs approximately 1 million people across all roles. Sanity-checking claimed records against market size data reveals inflated coverage claims that would result in bounce-heavy lists.

Dimension 4: Freshness and Compliance Documentation

Data freshness is the most commonly underdisclosed quality dimension. B2B contacts decay at 22 to 25% annually. Ask specifically: when was this data last verified? What is the re-verification cycle? Industry benchmark re-verification cycles: quarterly is the gold standard; semi-annual is acceptable for stable industries; annual re-verification is only adequate for government and legacy industrial sector contacts.

Legitimate B2B data providers document their data collection methodology and legal basis for processing. Ask for: the sources from which data was collected, the legal basis under which the provider processes the data, and the data retention and deletion policy. Providers who cannot provide this documentation are either operating without a clear legal framework or using data collection methods they prefer not to disclose. The Complete Evaluation Checklist

Before purchasing any B2B contact database: (1) request 200-record sample from your specific target segment; (2) run sample through NeverBounce or ZeroBounce — reject if more than 10% invalid; (3) ask for verification methodology, last verification date, and bounce rate guarantee; (4) request record counts by geography, industry, seniority, and company size — reject if provider cannot provide sub-segment counts; (5) ask for re-verification cycle and confirm it matches the decay rate of your target segment; (6) request data sourcing documentation for GDPR compliance purposes; (7) confirm replacement policy for records that bounce above the guaranteed rate.

Providers who pass all checks are operating to professional data quality standards. The verification sample result is the most important single data point — a provider claims about methodology, freshness, and coverage are only as reliable as the actual deliverability of the data they deliver.
