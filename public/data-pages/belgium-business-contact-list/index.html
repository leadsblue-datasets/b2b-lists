Belgium — Market Intelligence Notes

Belgium is a small country with outsized strategic importance — Brussels hosts the headquarters of the European Union, NATO, and over 1,400 international organisations and NGOs, making it one of the most important political and institutional B2B markets in the world. Antwerp is Europe's 2nd largest port and the global centre of the diamond trade; Ghent and Liège are significant industrial cities. The Belgian business community is trilingual (Dutch-speaking Flanders, French-speaking Wallonia, German-speaking East Cantons) and highly internationally oriented given its geographically central position in the EU.

Key Market Facts

  • Brussels hosts HQs of the EU, NATO, and 1,400+ international organisations
  • Antwerp handles 13% of all EU external maritime trade
  • Antwerp is the world's largest diamond trading centre (80%+ of global rough diamonds)
  • Belgium has the world's highest density of robotics use in manufacturing per worker
  • Belgium produces 220,000+ tonnes of chocolate annually — the highest per-capita

Cold Email Strategy for Belgium

Belgian B2B cold email requires language awareness — Dutch for Flemish companies, French for Wallonian companies, and English for Brussels-based EU institutions and multinationals. English is widely understood in business contexts across all regions. Belgian executives are pragmatic and process-oriented; emails that are clear, structured, and reference compliance or regulatory context (particularly relevant given the EU institutional context) perform well. The EU institutional sector in Brussels has highly specific procurement processes — direct cold email to EU officials is less effective than participating in formal tender processes.

Business Culture

Belgian business culture varies significantly by region — Flemish business culture is pragmatic, direct, and Northern European in style; Wallonian culture is warmer, more relationship-oriented, and Southern European in flavour. Brussels business culture, serving the EU institutional world, is international and English-dominant.

Compliance Note

Belgium follows GDPR, enforced by APD (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens / Autorité de Protection des Données — the Belgian Data Protection Authority, bilingual by law). The APD follows EDPB guidance allowing legitimate interest for B2B cold email to corporate addresses where professionally relevant. Belgium's Electronic Communications Act regulates commercial electronic communications. The APD is a moderately active enforcement authority.

Industry Context Professional Services and EU institutional services dominate Brussels. Trade & Logistics is most significant in Antwerp. Pharmaceutical & Biotech is significant — UCB, Solvay, and a dense biotech cluster around Leuven/UCB. Manufacturing (steel, glass, chemicals) concentrates in Wallonia and the Ghent corridor. ICT is growing in Flanders.

Belgium B2B Market — Research Notes

The Belgium B2B market has a distinct sector weighting that shapes how outreach campaigns should be structured. Transportation holds the largest share at 18% of registered business activity, followed by Retail & Commerce at 17% and Professional Services at 15%. These three sectors alone account for the majority of decision-making density — which means a campaign that addresses all three with sector-specific copy will outperform a single generic send to the full list by a significant margin.

Company size distribution in Belgium is weighted toward smaller businesses. The micro and SME segment (businesses with fewer than 100 employees) represents the majority of the database. This has a direct implication for copy strategy: messaging that positions your offer as a growth enabler, cost saver, or operational simplifier tends to outperform messaging built around enterprise features. Decision-makers in this size band are typically the same people who handle day-to-day operations — their attention is limited and their tolerance for abstract value propositions is low.

From a compliance standpoint, GDPR provides the governing framework for B2B email outreach in Belgium. In practice, this means every campaign should include clear sender identification, a functional opt-out mechanism, and copy that is demonstrably relevant to the recipient's professional context. These requirements are easily met within a well-structured cold email sequence — and they also happen to be the same practices that generate better response rates regardless of legal obligation.

Legal & Compliance

GDPR — Belgium Email Marketing Rules

Regulatory framework for commercial B2B email outreach in Belgium.

GDPR

GDPR (Regulation 2016/679) permits B2B outreach to business email addresses under legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f)). Include a clear unsubscribe link, honour opt-outs within 30 days, and document your legitimate interest assessment. Business email addresses at a corporate domain qualify as professional contact data.
