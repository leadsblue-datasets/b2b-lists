Country Intelligence

Romania — Market Intelligence Notes

Romania is Central-Eastern Europe's 2nd largest market after Poland, with a 19-million-person population and one of the EU's fastest economic growth rates. Bucharest is the commercial capital — a rapidly modernising city hosting the regional offices of many multinationals and a fast-growing technology sector. Cluj-Napoca has become Romania's technology capital — home to a world-class IT industry that produces more software developers per capita than almost any EU city, attracting offices from major global tech companies. Timișoara is a significant automotive and manufacturing hub near the Serbian border.

Key Market Facts

  • Romania produces more software engineers per capita than any EU country except the Baltics
  • Cluj-Napoca IT sector grew 300% in 10 years to employ 25,000+ software developers
  • Romania is the EU's largest grain exporter after France and Germany
  • Dacia (Renault subsidiary in Pitești) exports 90% of its vehicles — Europe's cheapest car brand
  • Bitdefender (Bucharest) is one of the world's top 5 cybersecurity companies

Cold Email Strategy for Romania

Romanian B2B cold email in English is highly effective for the technology sector, which is internationally oriented — Romanian developers and IT companies operate globally. For traditional industries and government-connected sectors, Romanian-language outreach with local references performs better. Cluj's tech community in particular is globally connected and responsive to international outreach. Romanian IT professionals are in high demand globally; companies recruiting or partnering with Romanian tech firms should expect competitive positioning.

Business Culture

Romanian business culture is a blend of Latin warmth and Eastern European pragmatism. Romanians are generally direct in professional contexts and respond well to straightforward value propositions. The tech community in particular operates with Western business norms. Traditional sectors are more hierarchical and relationship-dependent.

Compliance Note

Romania follows GDPR, enforced by ANSPDCP (Autoritatea Națională de Supraveghere a Prelucrării Datelor cu Caracter Personal). ANSPDCP applies legitimate interest for B2B cold email to corporate addresses where professionally relevant. Romania's implementation of GDPR is standard EU practice; ANSPDCP is a moderately active enforcement authority.

Retail is dominated by international chains but a growing domestic retail sector exists.

Romania B2B Market — Research Notes

Running the numbers on a Romania outbound campaign gives a useful baseline for expectation-setting. At 22% open rates and 4.3% reply rates — the industry averages for Romania B2B cold email — a 1,000-contact send generates roughly 220 opens and 43 replies. If your close rate on qualified responses is 20%, and your average contract value is $5,000, a single campaign of that scale generates $43,000 in expected pipeline. At 1.1M+ contacts, the full database gives you enough depth to run multiple non-overlapping campaigns sequentially.

Industry segmentation has a measurable impact on those numbers. Campaigns sent to a vertically consistent audience — exclusively technology companies, for instance, rather than a mixed-sector list — typically see reply rates 2–4 percentage points higher than unsegmented sends to the same contacts. This database classifies every contact by SIC industry code, making it straightforward to build those segments before importing into your sending platform.

The 47% decision-maker concentration in this dataset is the other key multiplier. A list with 47% decision-makers means 47 in every 100 replies come from people with purchasing authority — compared to a list with 35% decision-makers, where 65% of positive replies require an additional qualification call before any commercial conversation begins. That difference compounds across a full outbound programme and has a material effect on the efficiency of your sales development team.

GDPR — Romania Email Marketing Rules

Regulatory framework for commercial B2B email outreach in Romania.

GDPR

GDPR (Regulation 2016/679) permits B2B outreach to business email addresses under legitimate interest (Article 6(1)(f)). Include a clear unsubscribe link, honour opt-outs within 30 days, and document your legitimate interest assessment. Business email addresses at a corporate domain qualify as professional contact data.
