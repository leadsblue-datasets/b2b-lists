Analysis

Malta B2B Market — Research Notes

CAN-SPAM + GDPR Best Practice is the governing framework for commercial email in Malta. For B2B outreach to corporate email addresses — which is what this database supports — the practical requirement is that your communication is relevant to the recipient's professional role, your identity is clearly disclosed, and you include a functional opt-out mechanism. These conditions are met by any well-structured cold email sequence; they are not meaningfully more restrictive than running an outbound programme in the United States under CAN-SPAM.

The more substantive compliance requirement is data handling rather than the email itself. The contacts in this database were compiled from public business registries and opt-in data partnerships — sources that are documented and attributable. If you are running campaigns into EU-adjacent markets or to businesses with Malta operations that fall under cross-border data regulations, your data processor agreement with your email sending platform should reflect where contact data is stored and processed. Most major platforms (HubSpot, Salesforce, Instantly, Smartlead) offer EU data residency options.

Practically, the highest compliance risk in B2B outreach is not the send itself — it is ignoring opt-out requests or continuing to send after a company has indicated that contact should stop. Building a suppression list from your first campaign, adding unsubscribes within 48 hours, and not adding suppressed contacts to subsequent imports is both legally required and good sending hygiene. Bounce rates above 3% and spam complaint rates above 0.1% will damage your domain reputation independent of any regulatory exposure.
