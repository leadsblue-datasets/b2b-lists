Pakistan — Market Intelligence Notes

Pakistan is a 240-million-person economy with a large and growing IT services sector that has emerged as a significant global software outsourcing destination. Karachi is the financial and commercial capital — Pakistan's largest city and home to the Karachi Stock Exchange, major banks (HBL, MCB, UBL), and the country's largest industrial base. Lahore is the cultural capital and a significant commercial city with a growing technology cluster. Islamabad is the political capital with government-adjacent professional services. Pakistan's textile sector is globally significant — the country is the world's 4th largest cotton producer and a major exporter of cotton yarn, fabric, and garments.

Key Market Facts

  • Pakistan's IT exports exceeded $2.6 billion in FY2023 — a 25% year-on-year increase
  • Pakistan is the world's 4th largest cotton producer and largest cotton yarn exporter
  • Sialkot produces 70% of the world's hand-stitched footballs including all FIFA World Cup balls
  • Pakistan has one of the youngest populations globally — median age 22 years
  • Lahore's IT sector employs 300,000+ software professionals
KEY BUSINESS HUBS  ·  Karachi · Lahore (Punjab tech cluster) · Islamabad · Faisalabad (textile hub) · Sialkot (sports goods)

Cold Email Strategy for Pakistan

Pakistani B2B cold email in English is standard for the technology and professional services sectors, which are internationally oriented. Pakistani IT companies actively seek international clients through digital channels — the IT export sector is specifically targeting US, UK, and Australian companies. Response rates from Pakistani technology companies to professional outreach are strong given the export orientation of the sector.

Business Culture

Pakistani business culture is relationship-oriented, hierarchical, and Islamic-values-influenced. Trust is built through consistent follow-through and personal rapport. The IT sector, particularly in Lahore and Islamabad, operates with more Western business norms due to its international client focus. Ramadan and Eid significantly affect business pace.

Compliance Note

Pakistan's Personal Data Protection Act has been drafted and revised multiple times but was still pending final enactment as of 2025. Existing data protection provisions fall under PECA (Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act 2016) and sector-specific regulations. In practice, B2B cold email faces minimal regulatory enforcement risk in Pakistan currently.

Pakistan B2B Market — Research Notes

Running the numbers on a Pakistan outbound campaign gives a useful baseline for expectation-setting. At 28.5% open rates and 7.8% reply rates — the industry averages for Pakistan B2B cold email — a 1,000-contact send generates roughly 285 opens and 78 replies. If your close rate on qualified responses is 20%, and your average contract value is $5,000, a single campaign of that scale generates $78,000 in expected pipeline. At 79.5M+ contacts, the full database gives you enough depth to run multiple non-overlapping campaigns sequentially.

Industry segmentation has a measurable impact on those numbers. Campaigns sent to a vertically consistent audience — exclusively textile & manufacturing companies, for instance, rather than a mixed-sector list — typically see reply rates 2–4 percentage points higher than unsegmented sends to the same contacts. This database classifies every contact by SIC industry code, making it straightforward to build those segments before importing into your sending platform.

The 41% decision-maker concentration in this dataset is the other key multiplier. A list with 41% decision-makers means 41 in every 100 replies come from people with purchasing authority — compared to a list with 35% decision-makers, where 65% of positive replies require an additional qualification call before any commercial conversation begins. That difference compounds across a full outbound programme and has a material effect on the efficiency of your sales development team.
