B2B Data Glossary

Plain-English definitions of 30+ terms used in B2B email databases, cold email outreach, deliverability, and compliance. Updated 2026.

Verification

SMTP Verification
A method of confirming that an email address exists and can receive messages by simulating a connection to the recipient mail server without actually sending an email. The gold standard for real-time email validation.

Deliverability

Hard Bounce
A permanent email delivery failure caused by an invalid, non-existent, or blocked email address. Hard bounces above 2% damage sender reputation and should be removed from lists immediately after a single occurrence.
Soft Bounce
A temporary email delivery failure caused by a full inbox, server downtime, or message size limits. Soft bounces can be retried after 24-48 hours. Persistent soft bounces should be suppressed after 3-5 attempts.
Sender Reputation
A score assigned by email providers to sending domains and IP addresses based on engagement rates, bounce rates, spam complaint rates, and sending patterns. Poor sender reputation causes emails to land in spam regardless of content quality.
Inbox Placement Rate
The percentage of sent emails that land in the primary inbox rather than spam, promotions, or other folders. More meaningful than open rate as a deliverability metric. Tools like GlockApps and MailGenius test inbox placement before campaigns launch.

Technical

SPF (Sender Policy Framework)
A DNS record that specifies which mail servers are authorised to send email on behalf of your domain. Required configuration for legitimate cold email sending. Missing SPF records increase spam folder placement significantly.
DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail)
A cryptographic signature added to outgoing emails that allows receiving servers to verify the message was not altered in transit and originated from an authorised server. Pairs with SPF and DMARC for full authentication.
DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication)
A policy record that instructs receiving mail servers on how to handle emails that fail SPF or DKIM checks. Essential for protecting your domain from spoofing and improving deliverability.
Email Warm-Up
The process of gradually increasing sending volume from a new domain or email account to establish positive sender reputation before full-scale campaigns. Standard practice is starting at 20-30 emails/day and increasing by 20% per week over 4-6 weeks.
Suppression List
A list of email addresses that should never be contacted — hard bounces, opt-outs, spam complaints, and competitor domains. Maintaining a suppression list is required for CAN-SPAM compliance and essential for protecting sender reputation.
CSV (Comma-Separated Values)
The standard file format for email database delivery. Compatible with all major email sending platforms (Instantly, Smartlead, Apollo), CRM systems (HubSpot, Salesforce, Pipedrive), and spreadsheet applications (Excel, Google Sheets).

Data Quality

Decision-Maker
In B2B databases, a contact classified as having purchasing authority — typically business owners, directors, C-suite executives, and senior managers (VP level and above). Decision-maker ratio is a key quality metric; higher ratios produce better cold email response rates.
Email Verification Rate
The percentage of email addresses in a database that have been confirmed as valid and deliverable through SMTP or inbox testing. Quality databases target 95%+ verification rates. Anything below 90% introduces significant deliverability risk.
Data Freshness
The recency of contact information in a database. Email addresses decay at approximately 22-25% annually as professionals change jobs, companies close, and domains expire. Quarterly re-verification is the industry standard for maintaining freshness.
Double Opt-In
An email subscription process requiring subscribers to confirm their signup via a follow-up email. Produces higher-quality subscriber lists than single opt-in with lower bounce rates and higher engagement, but results in smaller list sizes.

Classification

SIC Code (Standard Industrial Classification)
A four-digit numerical code assigned by the US government to classify businesses by industry. Used extensively in B2B databases to segment contacts by sector. Superseded by NAICS in US government use but still widely used in B2B data.
NAICS Code (North American Industry Classification System)
The current US government standard for classifying business establishments by industry. Six-digit codes providing more granular industry classification than SIC. Common in newer B2B databases covering North American markets.
Company Firmographics
Business attributes used to segment B2B databases: industry, company size (employees or revenue), annual revenue, geographic location, founding year, and technology stack. Firmographic segmentation is the primary method for building targeted prospect lists.

Compliance

CAN-SPAM Act
The US federal law governing commercial email, enforced by the FTC. Requires accurate sender identification, honest subject lines, physical postal address, and a functional unsubscribe mechanism. Does not require prior consent for B2B cold email to business addresses.
GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
EU regulation governing personal data processing. For B2B email marketing, Article 6(1)(f) legitimate interest is the primary legal basis. Requires documented legitimate interest assessment, easy opt-out, and relevance to the recipient's professional role.
CASL (Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation)
Canada's anti-spam law requiring express or implied consent before sending commercial email. Implied consent exists for 24 months after a business relationship. One of the strictest email marketing laws globally.
Legitimate Interest
A GDPR legal basis (Article 6(1)(f)) permitting data processing when the data controller has a genuine business purpose that is not overridden by the data subject's rights. The standard legal basis for B2B cold email to business contacts in the EU.

Campaign Metrics

Open Rate
The percentage of delivered emails that were opened by recipients. B2B cold email averages 15-28% with personalised copy. Open rates are affected by subject line quality, sender name recognition, preview text, and send timing.
Reply Rate
The percentage of delivered emails that received a response. More meaningful than open rate for outbound sales. Industry average for B2B cold email is 2-5%. Campaigns with tight segmentation and relevant copy regularly achieve 8-15%.
Unsubscribe Rate
The percentage of email recipients who opt out of further communication. CAN-SPAM requires honouring unsubscribe requests within 10 business days. Rates above 0.5% indicate relevance problems with copy or targeting.

Strategy

Cold Email Sequence
A series of pre-written emails sent to prospects over several weeks with automated spacing. Typically 3-5 emails. First email presents the offer; follow-ups add value or change angle. Sequences outperform single-send campaigns by 2-3x on response rates.
Lead Generation
The process of identifying and attracting potential customers for a business. In B2B contexts, email outreach using purchased databases is one of the highest-ROI lead generation channels, particularly for companies targeting specific industries or geographies.
ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
A detailed description of the type of company that would receive maximum value from your product or service. Defined by industry, company size, geography, technology stack, and business model. Precise ICP definition is the foundation of effective database segmentation.

Data Sources

BuiltWith
A technology intelligence platform that identifies which technologies websites use by analysing site code, DNS records, and cookies. Used to build B2B databases of website owners filtered by specific technology adoption.

Data Types

B2C Email List
A database of personal (consumer) email addresses used for direct-to-consumer marketing. Distinct from B2B lists in that they target individuals as consumers rather than as business professionals. Subject to stricter consent requirements under GDPR and CASL.
