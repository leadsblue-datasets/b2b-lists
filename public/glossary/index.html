B2B Data Glossary

The complete reference glossary for B2B outreach professionals — covering data quality, cold email, email deliverability, compliance frameworks, and sales intelligence terminology. 100 terms, each with a precise, practitioner-focused definition.

B2B Email List
A structured database of verified business contact information — including email addresses, job titles, company names, and firmographic data — compiled for use in commercial outreach campaigns targeting professionals in their business capacity.
Verified Email Address
An email address confirmed to be deliverable through SMTP validation, inbox confirmation testing, or both. Verification checks whether the address exists on the mail server and whether delivery to the inbox is accepted.
SMTP Validation
A method of email verification that simulates a delivery connection to a mail server and checks whether a specific email address exists, without actually sending a message. The industry standard first-stage verification method.
Hard Bounce
A permanent email delivery failure caused by an invalid, non-existent, or blocked email address. Hard bounces above 2% of total sends damage sender domain reputation and reduce inbox placement rates across all subsequent campaigns.
Soft Bounce
A temporary email delivery failure caused by a full mailbox, a temporarily unavailable server, or a message size rejection. Unlike hard bounces, soft bounces do not indicate a permanently invalid address.
Data Decay Rate
The percentage of B2B contact records that become invalid or outdated within a given period, typically measured annually. The industry average is 22 to 25% per year, with technology sector contacts decaying at 28 to 38% annually.
Deliverability Rate
The percentage of sent emails that are successfully delivered to recipient mail servers — distinct from inbox placement rate, which measures what percentage land in the inbox rather than the spam folder. Target deliverability rate: 97%+.
Inbox Placement Rate
The percentage of delivered emails that land in the recipient inbox (primary tab or main inbox) versus the spam or promotions folder. Measured using seed testing tools such as GlockApps or MailReach. Target: 85%+.
Bounce Rate
The percentage of sent emails that fail to deliver, expressed as a ratio of total sends. Hard bounce rate above 2% triggers deliverability penalties from major email providers. Combined bounce rates above 5% indicate serious list quality problems.
Email List Cleaning
The process of removing invalid, inactive, duplicate, and role-based email addresses from a contact list before sending. Typically performed using verification services (NeverBounce, ZeroBounce, Bouncer) that test each address for deliverability.
Data Enrichment
The process of appending additional data fields to existing contact records — adding company revenue, employee count, technology stack, LinkedIn URL, or other firmographic attributes to enhance targeting precision and personalisation depth.
Firmographic Data
Structured data attributes describing a company, analogous to demographic data for individuals. Key firmographic fields: industry classification (SIC/NAICS), employee count, annual revenue, geographic location, company age, and public vs private status.
Technographic Data
Data indicating which technology platforms, software, or tools a company uses — their technology stack. Compiled by platforms like BuiltWith or G2, technographic data enables targeting by technology adoption (e.g. companies using Salesforce, Shopify, or HubSpot).
Intent Data
Behavioural signals indicating that a company or individual is actively researching or considering a purchase in a specific category. Intent data sources include content consumption data, search activity, review site visits, and third-party intent networks.
ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
A detailed description of the type of company that is the best fit for your product or service — the organisation most likely to buy, derive maximum value, and remain a long-term customer. ICP typically includes industry, size, geography, and technology characteristics.
TAM (Total Addressable Market)
The total revenue opportunity available if 100% of the defined target market were captured. In B2B data context, the total count of companies matching the ideal customer profile across all geographies the company serves.
SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)
The portion of TAM that a company can realistically target given its current product capabilities, geographic reach, and go-to-market resources. SAM is typically a subset of TAM after filtering by practical constraints.
Data Provenance
The documented origin and collection history of data — which sources were used to compile a contact database, how data was collected, and how it has been processed or transformed since collection. Critical for GDPR compliance assessment.
Contact Decay
The gradual degradation of contact record accuracy over time as professionals change jobs, companies change domains, and email formats are updated. Measured as the annual percentage of records that become inaccurate or undeliverable.
Suppression List
A maintained list of email addresses that must not receive outreach — including opt-outs, existing customers, current prospects, and complainants. All purchased lists should be screened against the suppression list before any sending.
Cold Email
Unsolicited commercial email sent to business contacts who have not previously opted in to receive communications from the sender. Distinguished from spam by relevance, compliance, and personalisation — and legally permissible under CAN-SPAM, GDPR legitimate interest, and most B2B frameworks.
Reply Rate
The percentage of cold email sends that generate a response from the recipient. Positive reply rate (genuine interest only, excluding opt-outs and auto-replies) is the most meaningful performance metric. Industry benchmark: 2.5 to 6% for well-targeted B2B cold email.
Open Rate
The percentage of delivered emails that are opened by the recipient. A proxy for subject line quality and deliverability. Cold email open rate benchmark: 18 to 26% for US B2B outreach with segmented copy. Open rates may be inflated by email client image prefetching.
Cadence
The timing and frequency structure of a multi-touch outreach sequence — the number of contact attempts, the intervals between them, and the channels used. A standard B2B cold email cadence: 4 emails over 18 to 20 days at 5 to 6 day intervals.
Sequence
A structured series of automated outreach messages sent to a prospect over a defined period, typically including 3 to 5 email touches and optionally LinkedIn or phone touches. Most cold email platforms (Instantly, Smartlead) support multi-step automated sequences.
Breakup Email
The final email in a cold outreach sequence, explicitly framing it as the last contact attempt and inviting the prospect to reach out when timing improves. Breakup emails consistently generate the highest reply rates in a sequence by creating urgency and finality.
Inbox Rotation
A cold email infrastructure technique where outbound emails are distributed across multiple sending mailboxes and domains, reducing the volume any single inbox must send and lowering the risk of reputation damage from high-volume sending.
Email Warm-Up
The process of gradually increasing the sending volume of a new email inbox or domain to build positive sender reputation before scaling to full campaign volumes. Typically takes 30 to 45 days and is automated by platforms like Instantly and Smartlead.
Domain Warm-Up
The process of establishing positive sender reputation for a new sending domain before using it for cold email campaigns. Involves sending low volumes of emails to engaged recipients over 4 to 6 weeks, gradually increasing volume as reputation builds.
Sender Score
A numerical rating (0 to 100) of an IP address sender reputation, aggregated from recipient feedback loops, complaint rates, and spam trap hits. Scores above 80 indicate good reputation; below 70 signals deliverability problems.
Sending Domain
A domain registered specifically for cold email outreach, separate from the primary business domain. Example: company-outreach.com used for prospecting while company.com is protected for customer and internal communications.
Spam Complaint Rate
The percentage of email recipients who mark a message as spam. Target below 0.08% for cold email. Above 0.1% triggers automated inbox filtering adjustments at Gmail and Outlook, reducing inbox placement for subsequent sends on that domain.
Spam Trap
An email address specifically created or repurposed to catch senders who do not maintain list hygiene. Pristine spam traps are addresses never used to sign up for anything; recycled spam traps are abandoned addresses recaptured by providers. Hitting a spam trap is a serious sender reputation signal.
A/B Testing
In cold email, the practice of sending two variants of an email (subject line, copy, CTA, send time) to split halves of a contact segment simultaneously to identify which version achieves higher open or reply rates. Requires minimum 100 opens per variant for statistical significance.
SDR (Sales Development Representative)
A sales role focused on outbound prospecting and lead qualification — identifying, contacting, and qualifying potential buyers before handing them to Account Executives for further sales conversations. SDRs are the primary users of cold email and B2B data platforms.
BDR (Business Development Representative)
A role similar to SDR, typically focused on outbound prospecting for new market development, partnerships, or enterprise account development. The distinction from SDR varies by company; both roles are heavy consumers of B2B contact data and cold email tools.
Multi-Channel Outreach
A prospecting approach that uses multiple communication channels — email, LinkedIn, phone, direct mail, or social — to reach the same prospect across multiple touch points. Multi-channel sequences consistently outperform single-channel approaches by 20 to 35% on conversion rates.
Account-Based Marketing (ABM)
A B2B marketing strategy that focuses resources on a specific set of target accounts rather than broad market prospecting. ABM treats individual companies as markets of one, delivering highly personalised outreach to multiple stakeholders within each target account.
BANT
A sales qualification framework: Budget (does the prospect have budget?), Authority (does the contact have purchasing authority?), Need (does the company have a problem your solution solves?), Timeline (when will they make a decision?). Used to qualify cold email replies before committing sales resources.
MEDDIC
An enterprise sales qualification methodology: Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion. Used to structure enterprise deal qualification and ensure alignment between seller understanding and buyer reality before significant sales resource investment.
Champion
The internal stakeholder at a prospect company who advocates for your solution within their organisation, builds the business case, and drives the internal evaluation process. Champions are the most important relationship in any B2B deal — they enable access to economic buyers and navigate internal blockers.
Economic Buyer
The person at a prospect company who has final budget authority and signs the purchase contract. The economic buyer is not always the day-to-day contact or champion — reaching them through internal champions is typically more effective than cold outreach directly to the economic buyer.
Pipeline Velocity
A measure of how quickly deals move through the sales pipeline, calculated as: (number of deals x win rate x average deal size) / sales cycle length. Cold email campaigns that generate high-quality leads improve pipeline velocity by feeding qualified, fast-moving opportunities.
Touch Point
A single interaction between a salesperson or marketer and a prospect — one email, one LinkedIn message, one phone call, one piece of direct mail. Most B2B deals require 8 to 12 touch points before a decision. Cold email sequences are designed to deliver the required touch points systematically.
CAN-SPAM Act
The US federal law governing commercial email. Key requirements: no deceptive subject lines, clear sender identification, valid physical address, functional opt-out link honoured within 10 business days. No prior consent required for B2B cold email to US recipients.
GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
The EU data protection regulation governing the processing of personal data of EU residents. For B2B email outreach, GDPR Article 6(1)(f) — legitimate interest — is the lawful basis permitting cold email to business email addresses associated with a professional role.
Legitimate Interest
A lawful basis under GDPR Article 6(1)(f) that permits processing of personal data where necessary for genuine business purposes, provided the controller interests are not overridden by the data subject rights. Requires a documented three-part Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA).
LIA (Legitimate Interest Assessment)
A documented three-part analysis required when relying on legitimate interest as a GDPR lawful basis: (1) Purpose Test — is there a legitimate interest?, (2) Necessity Test — is processing necessary for that interest?, (3) Balancing Test — do controller interests override data subject interests?
CASL (Canadian Anti-Spam Legislation)
Canadian law governing commercial electronic messages. Requires express or implied consent. Implied consent applies for 24 months after a commercial transaction or where an email address is conspicuously published for commercial purposes and outreach is relevant to the published role.
PECR (Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations)
UK regulations implementing the ePrivacy Directive that govern the sending of electronic marketing messages to individuals. B2B cold email to corporate email addresses (where the company is the subscriber) is permitted on an opt-out basis under PECR.
PDPA (Personal Data Protection Act)
Singapore data protection legislation permitting B2B cold email where the contact is acting in their professional business capacity and the communication is related to their business functions. Requires clear sender identification and a functional opt-out mechanism.
LGPD (Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados)
Brazil data protection law modelled on GDPR. Legitimate interest applies as a lawful basis for B2B email outreach to professional contacts. Requires a documented legitimate interest assessment and a functional opt-out mechanism honoured within 15 days.
Data Subject Rights
Rights granted to individuals under GDPR and similar frameworks: the right of access (to know what data is held), right to erasure (right to be forgotten), right to rectification (correction of inaccurate data), right to portability, and right to object to processing.
Consent Management
Systems and processes for obtaining, recording, and managing data subjects consent for data processing and communications. For B2B outreach relying on legitimate interest, formal consent management is not required, but suppression list management (honouring opt-outs) is mandatory.
Data Controller
Under GDPR, the entity that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data. If you purchase a B2B database and use it for your outreach campaigns, you are the data controller for that processing activity and bear compliance responsibility.
DPA (Data Processing Agreement)
A contract between a data controller and a data processor that defines the terms under which the processor handles personal data on behalf of the controller. Required under GDPR when a controller shares personal data with a third-party processor.
Double Opt-In
An email subscription confirmation process where a subscriber must confirm their email address by clicking a link in a confirmation email. Not required for B2B cold email outreach; relevant for newsletter and marketing email list building to ensure list quality and compliance.
DSAR (Data Subject Access Request)
A formal request from an individual exercising their right of access under GDPR or similar frameworks — requesting to know what personal data the organisation holds about them. Organisations must respond to DSARs within 30 days (GDPR) or the applicable national timeframe.
Opt-Out
A mechanism allowing email recipients to remove themselves from future communications. CAN-SPAM requires opt-out to be honoured within 10 business days. GDPR requires it immediately (within 30 days maximum). An opt-out adds the contact permanently to the suppression list.
Implied Consent
Consent inferred from circumstances rather than explicitly given. Under CASL, implied consent exists where a contact published their email in a business context, the message is relevant to that context, and no prior opt-out has been recorded. Valid for 24 months from the initial commercial transaction.
SPF (Sender Policy Framework)
A DNS-based email authentication mechanism that specifies which IP addresses and mail servers are authorised to send email on behalf of a domain. Required for inbox placement at Gmail and Outlook. Configured as a TXT record in DNS.
DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail)
A cryptographic email authentication method that adds a digital signature to outgoing messages, allowing receiving mail servers to verify the message was sent by an authorised sender and was not modified in transit. Required for inbox placement alongside SPF.
DMARC
A DNS-based email authentication policy that specifies what receiving mail servers should do when SPF and/or DKIM checks fail. Three policies: p=none (monitoring only), p=quarantine (send to spam), p=reject (block delivery). Required at p=quarantine or p=reject for BIMI compliance.
BIMI (Brand Indicators for Message Identification)
An email standard that displays a brand logo in the inbox alongside verified sender email. Requires DMARC at p=quarantine or p=reject, an active domain, and a verified mark certificate (VMC). Improves brand recognition and inbox trust signals.
Domain Reputation
A score assigned to a sending domain by email providers (Gmail, Outlook) based on historical sending behaviour — bounce rates, spam complaint rates, engagement rates, and spam trap hits. Domain reputation directly affects inbox placement across all campaigns from that domain.
IP Reputation
A score assigned to a sending IP address based on historical behaviour. Unlike domain reputation, IP reputation is shared across all senders using the same IP (on shared IP infrastructure). Dedicated IPs allow full ownership of IP reputation.
Feedback Loop (FBL)
A service offered by major inbox providers that forwards spam complaint data to the sending domain owner when a recipient marks an email as spam. Gmail and Outlook provide FBL data through Google Postmaster Tools and Microsoft SNDS respectively.
MX Record
A DNS record specifying the mail server(s) responsible for accepting email on behalf of a domain. MX record lookups are the first step in email delivery. Invalid or missing MX records result in hard bounces for the entire domain.
Blacklist
A list of IP addresses or domains identified as sources of spam or abusive email, maintained by organisations like Spamhaus or Barracuda. Appearing on a major blacklist results in email delivery failures at providers that check the blacklist. Removal (delisting) requires resolving the underlying issue.
Google Postmaster Tools
A free service from Google that provides Gmail-specific deliverability data for verified sending domains: domain reputation rating, spam rate, delivery errors, and authentication compliance. The most important free deliverability monitoring tool for senders with Gmail-heavy recipient lists.
Catch-All Email Address
An email address configured to accept all messages sent to any address at a domain, regardless of whether that specific address exists. Catch-all addresses cannot be validated by SMTP verification. Most verification tools mark them as risky or accept-all status.
Role-Based Email Address
Generic email addresses representing a function rather than an individual — info@, support@, sales@, admin@, contact@. Role-based addresses should be excluded from cold outreach lists as they are managed by multiple people, have high spam filter sensitivity, and generate disproportionate complaint rates.
Email Authentication
The set of technical protocols (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) that verify the legitimacy of an email sender and the integrity of the message. All three authentication protocols must be correctly configured for optimal inbox placement at Gmail and Outlook.
Dedicated IP Address
An IP address used exclusively by one sender for email delivery. Dedicated IPs provide full control and accountability for IP reputation but require volume management and warm-up. Recommended for senders above 100,000 emails per month or those with highly sensitive deliverability requirements.
Sales Intelligence Platform
A software platform that combines B2B contact data, company intelligence, and workflow tools to support prospecting and outreach. Examples: ZoomInfo, Apollo.io, Cognism, Lusha. Distinguished from databases (which provide static data exports) by their real-time search and workflow capabilities.
Revenue Intelligence
Technology that captures, analyses, and provides insights from customer and prospect interactions (calls, emails, meetings) to improve forecasting accuracy, deal velocity, and sales process adherence. Examples: Gong, Chorus, Clari.
Win/Loss Analysis
A systematic process of reviewing closed deals (both won and lost) to identify patterns in what factors contributed to each outcome. Win/loss data informs ICP refinement, competitive positioning, and sales process improvement.
Net Revenue Retention (NRR)
A metric measuring the revenue retained from existing customers after accounting for expansion (upsell/cross-sell), contraction (downgrades), and churn over a period. NRR above 100% indicates expansion revenue exceeds churn losses. Target for healthy SaaS: above 110%.
Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
The total cost of acquiring one new customer — including sales and marketing salaries, data costs, platform costs, and campaign spend, divided by the number of new customers acquired in the period. CAC must be substantially lower than Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) for sustainable growth.
Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)
The total revenue expected from a customer relationship over its entire duration. CLV-to-CAC ratio above 3:1 indicates sustainable unit economics for B2B SaaS. CLV informs how much can be spent on cold email outreach and data acquisition to acquire each customer.
Land and Expand
A B2B sales strategy where the initial sale is a smaller, lower-friction deal that establishes a customer relationship, followed by expansion into additional products, seats, or use cases. Cold email outreach can support land-and-expand by targeting individual departments before enterprise-wide deals.
Lookalike Audience
A prospect list built by identifying companies or contacts that share attributes with existing best customers. B2B lookalike audiences are generated by matching firmographic, technographic, and behavioural attributes of high-value customers against database records to find similar prospects.
Mutual Action Plan (MAP)
A shared document between a salesperson and a buyer that defines the steps, responsibilities, and timeline required to evaluate and purchase a solution. MAPs reduce deal slippage by creating joint accountability and visibility into the buyer evaluation process.
Buying Committee
The group of stakeholders at a prospect company who collectively influence or make a B2B purchasing decision. Typically includes an economic buyer, champion, user buyer, and technical influencer. Understanding the buying committee structure is essential for complex enterprise deal navigation.
Vendor Evaluation
The formal process by which a B2B buyer assesses and compares vendors before making a purchase decision. Includes demonstrations, trials, reference checks, security reviews, and legal review. Understanding the evaluation process a prospect uses helps sellers support the right steps at the right time.
Data Quality Score
A composite metric assessing the overall quality of a contact database, typically incorporating deliverability rate, completeness (percentage of records with all required fields populated), freshness (time since last verification), and accuracy (match rate against reference sources).
Email Validation
The process of determining whether an email address is correctly formatted, exists on the mail server, and is likely to deliver successfully. Validation includes syntax checking, domain validation, MX record lookup, and SMTP connection testing.
Job Title Normalisation
The process of standardising diverse job title variants into consistent, comparable categories. Example: normalising VP of Marketing, Vice President Marketing, VP Marketing, and VP Mktg into a single Director-level Marketing category for segmentation and targeting purposes.
Data Lineage
The documented history of how data has been sourced, transformed, and moved between systems. For B2B contact databases, data lineage documentation establishes that contacts were sourced from legitimate public sources rather than scraped from non-consenting sources.
Cohort Analysis
The analysis of a defined group (cohort) of contacts over time to understand behaviour patterns, decay rates, or performance characteristics. In B2B data, cohort analysis identifies which contact segments decay fastest and which data sources produce the most durable records.
Attribution
The process of crediting a specific marketing or sales activity with responsibility for a business outcome (lead, meeting, revenue). Multi-touch attribution models distribute credit across multiple touchpoints in the buyer journey, rather than crediting only the first or last touch.
Benchmark Data
Industry-standard performance metrics used as reference points for evaluating campaign results. Cold email benchmarks include open rates, reply rates, bounce rates, and spam complaint rates by industry, geography, and job seniority. B2B Data Index publishes benchmark data at b2bdataindex.com/benchmarks/.
Segmentation
The process of dividing a contact list into groups based on shared characteristics — industry, company size, geography, seniority, technology stack, or buying behaviour. Segmented campaigns consistently outperform unsegmented campaigns by 30 to 50% on reply rates.
Personalisation
The practice of customising outreach content to the specific characteristics, context, or behaviour of the recipient. Personalisation levels range from first-name token insertion (minimal lift) to role-specific pain point references and company-specific observations (40 to 80% reply rate lift).
List Segmentation
The division of a contact database into targeted subsets based on defined criteria before campaign deployment. The most impactful segmentation variable for cold email is industry vertical, followed by company size, then geography. Segmentation before sending prevents generic copy that reduces reply rates.
Research Section
On B2B Data Index, the /research/ section contains four citable, Dataset-schema-marked research reports on B2B data quality, cold email benchmarks, compliance landscape, and decision-maker profiles. Each report is available as structured JSON for citation and data integration.
Standards Section
On B2B Data Index, the /standards/ section defines the technical and operational standards for B2B data quality, cold email performance benchmarks, compliance documentation, and list verification. These standards define what "good" looks like for each dimension of B2B outreach.
95% Deliverability Standard
The B2B Data Index minimum deliverability standard for verified databases. A 95% deliverability rate means at most 5% of records in a database produce delivery failures. This standard protects campaign sender reputation and is verified through SMTP validation and inbox confirmation testing.
Quarterly Verification Cycle
The data refresh standard applied to B2B databases at LeadsBlue and cataloged on B2B Data Index. Every database is re-verified through SMTP validation at minimum once per quarter, ensuring that contacts reflecting job changes, company closures, and domain changes are removed from active databases.
Decision-Maker Ratio
The percentage of records in a B2B database that represent contacts with purchasing authority or significant buying influence (Directors and above). B2B Data Index databases target a decision-maker ratio of 38 to 52%, ensuring that outbound campaigns reach contacts positioned to act on or recommend solutions.
