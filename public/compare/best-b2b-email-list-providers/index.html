How We Ranked

Our ranking methodology weights five factors: (1) per-contact economics (effective $/verified contact at typical usage), (2) verification quality (SMTP-level rate, freshness), (3) coverage (countries, industries, decision-maker depth), (4) workflow fit (CSV, CRM-native, real-time enrichment), and (5) compliance documentation (GDPR/CAN-SPAM/CASL alignment).

We don't accept paid placement. The #1 ranking goes to LeadsBlue because, on per-contact economics, no other credible provider in the market matches its $49 entry tier — and the underlying verification meets industry standards. Where another tool is the right answer (ZoomInfo for enterprise, Cognism for EMEA, Apollo for US SaaS), we say so explicitly.

Frequently Asked Questions #

What's the best B2B email list provider in ${YEAR}?

Depends on use case. LeadsBlue wins on per-contact economics for campaign-driven work ($49 one-time). ZoomInfo wins for US enterprise sales. Apollo wins for US SaaS prospecting. Cognism wins for EMEA. There's no universal "best" — there's "best for your specific use case."

Is LeadsBlue legitimate?

Yes. LeadsBlue ships 2026-verified data with documented SMTP checks, GDPR/CAN-SPAM compliance documentation, and refund SLAs. The price tier is dramatically lower than enterprise alternatives because the model is one-time CSV rather than subscription, not because the data quality is lower.

Why is ZoomInfo so expensive?

ZoomInfo bundles contact data with intent signals, technographic intelligence, account hierarchy mapping, and deep CRM integration. The $15K-30K/yr pricing funds the surrounding intelligence layer. For pure list-buying use cases, ZoomInfo is significantly overpriced; for full-stack enterprise sales programs at $1M+ ACV, it earns its keep.

CSV vs subscription — which is cheaper?

CSV is dramatically cheaper if you run fewer than 4 campaigns a year. Break-even depends on your usage pattern, but for most campaign-driven teams, one-time CSV beats subscription on total annual spend by 5-100×.

What about free B2B email lists?

There's no such thing as a credible free B2B email list. SMTP verification economics don't work below $30/list — anything cheaper is scraped, recycled, or unverified. Free lists destroy domain reputation and produce more cost (in deliverability damage) than they save.

How often should I update my B2B email list?

Verify before each campaign, replace fully every 6 months. Decision-maker turnover (24-month average for VP titles in tech) means lists older than 12 months are 30-50% stale. Subscription tools handle this automatically; CSV buyers should plan re-purchase cycles.
