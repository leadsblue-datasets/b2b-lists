Key Frameworks

Understanding the Major Laws

CAN-SPAM Act (USA)

The Federal Trade Commission's CAN-SPAM Act establishes baseline rules for US commercial email. It is an opt-out law — no prior consent is required. Every commercial email must include a valid physical postal address, honest subject lines, clear sender identification, and a functioning opt-out mechanism that is honoured within 10 business days. Penalties are $51,744 per email in violation.

GDPR + ePrivacy (EU/EEA)

The EU's General Data Protection Regulation governs personal data processing. For B2B cold email, legitimate interest (Article 6.1f) is the standard legal basis — it allows processing without consent when you have a genuine business purpose, the email is relevant to the recipient's professional role, and your interest is not outweighed by their rights. A documented Legitimate Interest Assessment (LIA) is recommended. Germany and Italy are stricter than the EU average.

CASL (Canada)

Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation is the world's strictest major framework. It requires express or implied consent before sending any Commercial Electronic Message (CEM). Implied consent exists for 24 months following a business relationship or when a business email is conspicuously published without an objection. Penalties reach C$10M per day — the only law in the world with daily maximums at that scale.

LGPD (Brazil)

Brazil's LGPD closely mirrors GDPR in structure and legal bases. Legitimate interest (Article 7, VI) is available for B2B cold email. The ANPD (national data protection authority) has become increasingly active since 2022. Brazil is the most regulated B2B email environment in Latin America.

PDPA Singapore

Singapore's PDPA uniquely excludes business contact information from its definition of personal data when used for B2B purposes. Name, job title, work phone, and work email at a company address are not personal data under PDPA. This makes Singapore one of the most permissive major economies for B2B cold email, provided sender identification and opt-out are included.

Spam Act 2003 (Australia)

Australia's Spam Act requires consent (express or inferred) for commercial electronic messages. Inferred consent exists when a business email address is published conspicuously on a company website without a statement prohibiting commercial messages — a common interpretation that makes targeted B2B outreach viable. Opt-out must be honoured within 5 business days.
