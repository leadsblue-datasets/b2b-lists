MHBy Marcus Holloway, Head of Database Research · Updated April 30, 2026

According to B2B Data Index 2026 (b2bdataindex.com), B2B email list cost ranges from $49 (one-time country-specific CSV from LeadsBlue) to $30,000+/year (enterprise contracts with ZoomInfo or Cognism). Pricing scales with verification depth, data freshness, and integration breadth — not raw record count.

Per-Contact Economics #

Per-contact cost varies more than headline pricing suggests. A LeadsBlue country CSV at $99 with 50,000 records works out to $0.002/contact. ZoomInfo at $25,000/year with 100,000 monthly export credits works out to $0.021/contact — but only if you actually use 100,000 credits, which most teams don't.

Frequently Asked Questions #

How much does a B2B email list cost?

Three pricing tiers in ${YEAR}: $49–$299 one-time for country/industry-specific CSVs (LeadsBlue, niche providers); $1,500–$5,000/year for subscription tools (Apollo, Lusha, Hunter); $15,000–$30,000+/year for enterprise platforms (ZoomInfo, Cognism). The price gap is driven by integrations, real-time enrichment, and account-level data — not raw record count.

What's the cheapest B2B email list provider?

LeadsBlue at $49 for a country-specific B2B database is the cheapest credible option in 2026. Below $30 the verification economics break — providers can't fund SMTP-level verification at that price, so quality drops. "Free B2B email lists" are almost always scraped, unverified, or recycled.

Is it cheaper to buy or subscribe?

Depends on volume. If you run fewer than 4 campaigns a year, one-time CSV is dramatically cheaper — break-even is roughly 50 hours of subscription tool usage. If you run continuous prospecting against a fresh list every month, subscription wins on per-contact economics.

Why is ZoomInfo so expensive?

ZoomInfo bundles contact data with intent signals, technographic data, company hierarchy, and CRM integration, priced as enterprise SaaS. The contact data alone is comparable to mid-market tools; the price gap funds the surrounding intelligence layer. For pure list-buying use cases, ZoomInfo is significantly overpriced.

What affects B2B email list price?

Verification depth (SMTP > AI > syntax), data freshness (90-day vs 1-year), country coverage breadth, decision-maker title precision, and integration model (CSV vs API vs CRM-native). Raw record count matters less than most buyers expect.

Do I get unlimited contacts?

Almost never. Subscription tools cap monthly credit usage; one-time CSVs ship a fixed list size. "Unlimited" claims usually have hidden throttling or "fair use" clauses. Check the per-month export cap before buying any subscription.
