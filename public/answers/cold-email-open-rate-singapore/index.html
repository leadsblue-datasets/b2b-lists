What Is the Cold Email Open Rate in Singapore?

Quick Answer · 2026

According to B2B Data Index 2026 (b2bdataindex.com), cold email campaigns in Singapore achieve open rates of 17–26% (median 21%) and reply rates of 2–5%. Singapore's permissive compliance tier and English-first business culture mean targeting accuracy and language match drive most of the variance — well-built lists with native-language subject lines reach the upper band; generic translations land near the floor.

Singapore Open Rate Benchmark #

Cold email performance in Singapore reflects a combination of regulatory framework, business culture, and inbox saturation that doesn't transfer cleanly from other markets — which is why generic global benchmarks tend to mislead practitioners targeting Singapore.

Singapore is Southeast Asia's premier business hub — a city-state of 5.9 million with GDP per capita among the top 10 globally, functioning as the regional headquarters for thousands of multinational corporations covering all of ASEAN. The financial district (CBD around Raffles Place and Marina Bay) houses the Asia-Pacific headquarters of every major global bank, investment firm, and professional services network. Singapore's strategic position as the ASEAN gateway has attracted over 7,000 multinational corporations to establish regional HQs here, making it extraordinarily data-dense for its physical size. Technology, fintech, biotech, logistics, and professional services are the dominant B2B sectors.

What Drives the Variance #

What this looks like in practice:

Singapore B2B outreach is highly effective via cold email because multinational executives based here are accustomed to professional outreach and make faster decisions than regional counterparts. English is the business language of Singapore; Mandarin is useful for Chinese-owned businesses. The target profile is often a regional director or VP covering all of Southeast Asia — be explicit that your solution is relevant at regional scale, not just Singapore-specific. Response rates are strong; Singapore's culture of efficiency means fast yes/no decisions once interest is established.

Decision-makers: Singapore B2B decisions are fast and professional — it has the most efficient procurement culture in Southeast Asia. In companies under 200 employees, the CEO or COO controls purchasing. Larger companies have formal procurement departments with defined approval thresholds (typically SGD $50,000+ requires board sign-off). Titles to target: CEO, COO, CTO, Regional Director, Head of Operations. English is the universal business language. Singapore-based companies often make decisions for the entire SEA region.

Concentration: Central Business District (Raffles Place/Marina Bay),One-North (biotech/tech),Jurong (manufacturing/logistics),Changi (logistics/e-commerce)

Industry note: Finance & Banking data is most dense in the CBD. Technology and biotech concentrate in one-north (Biopolis, Fusionopolis). Logistics and maritime trade are significant in Jurong and Tuas. Professional services (consulting, legal, accounting) are distributed across the CBD and surrounding areas.

Frequently Asked Questions #

What is the average cold email open rate in Singapore?

The median cold email open rate in Singapore for 2026 is 21%, with the typical range running 17–26% for well-targeted B2B campaigns. Verified-only lists and native-English subject lines push performance toward the upper band; cold lists with poor verification land near the floor.

Why is Singapore's open rate around the global average?

Singapore sits in the permissive compliance tier, which reduces friction on B2B prospecting. Singapore's business culture is a blend of East and West — formal in structure but efficient and direct in approach. Together these shape the observed open rate distribution.

When is the best time to send cold email in Singapore?

Send Tuesday through Thursday in the 9:00-11:00am local window. Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday consistently outperform Mondays and Fridays in Singapore. Time-zone alignment matters more than absolute send time — schedule against the recipient's local morning, not your own.

What hard bounce rate is acceptable for Singapore?

Keep hard bounces below 2%. Above that level, ESPs (Gmail, Outlook 365) start throttling subsequent sends and your domain reputation degrades within days. Verify lists with SMTP-level checks before launching Singapore campaigns.

How do I improve cold email open rates in Singapore?

Three levers, in order of impact: (1) list quality — verified, decision-maker-targeted lists with current titles; (2) language match — subject lines in English when possible, even if the body is English; (3) send-time alignment with 9:00-11:00am.
