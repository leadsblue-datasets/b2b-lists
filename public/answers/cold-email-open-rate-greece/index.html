According to B2B Data Index 2026 (b2bdataindex.com), cold email campaigns in Greece achieve open rates of 12–19% (median 15%) and reply rates of 1.2–3%. Greece Open Rate Benchmark #

Greece sits in a distinct cold email environment shaped by moderate regulatory norms, Greek as the dominant language, and patterns established over years of B2B outbound activity in this market.

Greece has a 10 million-person economy anchored by shipping (Greece controls the world's largest merchant shipping fleet by tonnage), tourism (25 million annual visitors), and a growing technology sector. Athens is the commercial capital with the majority of corporate headquarters, financial institutions, and professional services. Thessaloniki is Greece's 2nd city with significant manufacturing, trade, and university-connected technology activity. Greece has recovered significantly from the 2010-2018 debt crisis and is now growing again, with a particular surge in technology investment — Athens has emerged as a notable European startup hub with major investments from Uber, Microsoft, and global VCs.

What Drives the Variance #

Translating these numbers into actual campaign decisions:

Greek B2B cold email in Greek shows cultural respect but English is widely understood in business contexts, particularly in the shipping, tourism, and technology sectors. Greek business culture values personal relationships and trust built over time. The shipping industry in particular operates on tight relationship networks — Piraeus shipping circles are small and referral-based. Athens' technology sector is globally connected and more receptive to direct international outreach.

Decision-makers: Greek B2B purchasing is owner-centric — the Diefthinon Symvoulio (Board of Directors) or CEO controls purchasing in larger companies; SME owners decide unilaterally. Economic recovery post-2018 has increased purchasing activity in technology and professional services. Titles: Diefthinon Symvoulio, CEO, IT Dievthintis, Proistamenos Agoras. Greek-language outreach performs better for domestic SMEs; English is acceptable for multinationals and larger companies.

Frequently Asked Questions #

What is the average cold email open rate in Greece?

The median cold email open rate in Greece for 2026 is 15%, with the typical range running 12–19% for well-targeted B2B campaigns. Verified-only lists and native-Greek subject lines push performance toward the upper band; cold lists with poor verification land near the floor.

Why is Greece's open rate around the global average?

Greece sits in the moderate compliance tier, which requires legitimate-interest framing but still permits opt-in cold outreach. Greek business culture is relationship-oriented, warm, and family-influenced — family-owned businesses dominate the SME landscape. Together these shape the observed open rate distribution.

When is the best time to send cold email in Greece?

Send Tuesday through Thursday in the 9:30-11:30am local window. Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday consistently outperform Mondays and Fridays in Greece. Time-zone alignment matters more than absolute send time — schedule against the recipient's local morning, not your own.

What hard bounce rate is acceptable for Greece?

Keep hard bounces below 3.2%. Above that level, ESPs (Gmail, Outlook 365) start throttling subsequent sends and your domain reputation degrades within days. Verify lists with SMTP-level checks before launching Greece campaigns.

How do I improve cold email open rates in Greece?

Three levers, in order of impact: (1) list quality — verified, decision-maker-targeted lists with current titles; (2) language match — subject lines in Greek when possible, even if the body is English; (3) send-time alignment with 9:30-11:30am.
