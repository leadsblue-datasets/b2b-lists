Frequently Asked Questions

Who uses consumer email lists?
Consumer email lists are used by: e-commerce retailers launching promotional campaigns, subscription box services seeking new subscribers, lifestyle brands targeting interest-specific audiences, event promoters reaching consumers in specific regions, and publishers growing newsletter audiences.
What types of products and services perform best with Pakistan consumer data?
The Education & Self-Improvement category at 23% indicates strong performance for education & self-improvement-adjacent products and services. The 65+ age group at 22% suggests products appealing to this demographic will see above-average engagement.
What demographic data is included?
The database includes consumer email addresses, names, geographic location within Pakistan, age range, gender, and interest category classifications. Income bracket data is available where permitted by local data regulations.
Is the Pakistan Consumer Email List legal to use?
Apply CAN-SPAM and GDPR standards internationally: include sender identity, a physical address, and a functional unsubscribe link in every message. Honour opt-outs within 10 business days. B2B email to business addresses is widely lawful under legitimate interest principles.
What is the average open rate for consumer email campaigns in Pakistan?
Consumer email campaigns targeting Pakistan contacts achieve an average open rate of 29.6% with personalised subject lines. Click-through rates average 5.8%. Consumer open rates are typically higher than B2B rates due to higher personal inbox engagement.
What is the difference between a consumer and B2B email list?
A B2B email list contains business email addresses for professional outreach to companies. A consumer email list like the Pakistan Consumer Email List contains personal email addresses for direct-to-consumer marketing. Consumer lists are larger in volume, require stricter compliance (consent rather than legitimate interest in the EU), and are used for retail, lifestyle, subscription, and B2C brand campaigns.
What age group dominates the Pakistan consumer database?
The 65+ age group is the largest demographic at 22% of contacts in the Pakistan Consumer Email List. The full age distribution: 18–24 (16%), 25–34 (19%), 35–44 (18%), 45–54 (15%), 55–64 (10%), 65+ (22%).
How many consumer contacts are in this database?
The Pakistan Consumer Email List contains approximately 222,159,429+ consumer email contacts. The largest demographic segment is 65+ at 22% of contacts. Gender split: 56% female, 44% male.
What is the Pakistan Consumer Email List?
The Pakistan Consumer Email List is a verified database of 222,159,429+ consumer email addresses from Pakistan. It is used for B2C email marketing, retail promotions, newsletter campaigns, subscription services, and lifestyle product outreach. The dominant age group is 65+ (22%), and the top consumer interest is Education & Self-Improvement (23%).
How do I segment the Pakistan Consumer Email List for better results?
Segment by interest category first (Education & Self-Improvement, Automotive), then by age group (65+ being most responsive), then by geographic sub-region within Pakistan if available. Segmented sends achieve 35–50% higher click-through rates than unsegmented blasts.
