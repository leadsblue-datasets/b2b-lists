# B2B Data Index

**A research and intelligence platform for B2B contact databases across 200+ countries.**

[b2bdataindex.com](https://b2bdataindex.com) helps sales teams, marketers, and growth operators evaluate business email databases before buying — with industry breakdowns, outreach benchmarks, compliance guidance, and data field documentation for each market.

---

## Why This Exists

Finding reliable B2B contact data for a specific country or industry is harder than it should be. Most providers give you a lead count and a price. You have no idea what sectors are represented, how the data is structured, what compliance framework applies to your campaign, or whether the fields you actually need (job title, phone, SIC code) are populated at anything near 100%.

B2B Data Index was built to answer those questions before you commit to a purchase. Each database entry documents what's in the dataset, who it's for, and what you can realistically do with it.

The underlying data is sourced and maintained by [LeadsBlue](https://leadsblue.com), one of the longest-running B2B contact database providers, with coverage spanning 140+ geographies and dozens of industry verticals.

## Compliance and Responsible Use

B2B contact data operates under different legal frameworks depending on where your recipients are located. This isn't optional reading — sending commercial email without understanding the applicable law creates real legal and deliverability risk.

Quick reference:

| Jurisdiction | Applicable Law | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| United States | CAN-SPAM Act | Physical address + unsubscribe link in every email |
| European Union | GDPR Article 6(1)(f) | Legitimate interest basis; honour opt-outs within 30 days |
| United Kingdom | UK GDPR + PECR | Same as EU GDPR for corporate emails; stricter for individuals |
| Canada | CASL | Implied consent lasts 24 months from last transaction |
| Australia | Spam Act 2003 | Consent required; unsubscribe honoured within 5 business days |

Every database page on b2bdataindex.com includes the specific compliance framework for that geography. The guide [Is Buying Email Lists Legal?](https://b2bdataindex.com/blog/is-buying-email-lists-legal.html) covers this in more depth.
